What we're building

Glow turns skincare into a daily habit you actually want to keep — quick check-ins, streaks, and small wins. Acne isn't a flaw to hide from the camera. It's just skin, and Glow treats it that way from the very first screen: no filters, no "flawless skin" pressure, no shame spirals.

Your skin has its own love language — what calms it, what sets it off, how it shifts with your cycle, stress, and the seasons. Glow learns that language with you over time, so the more you show up, the smarter your routine gets.

And we're not into guessing. Every recommendation comes with the ingredient science behind it, so you're not just following a routine — you're learning your skin, ingredient by ingredient.

We're building this in the open with our community. This page is our very first home on the web — and if you sign up below, you'll be some of the first people to get in.

Why we lead with acne positivity

85%
of teenagers experience acne at some point — and more severe acne is linked to higher rates of anxiety and depression, especially among teen girls.
American Academy of Dermatology; adolescent mental health research

If something affects that many people, it isn't a personal failure — it's just skin. So Glow doesn't open with "fix this." It opens with "let's understand this together": no filters, no before/afters, no flawless-skin fantasy.

Acne positivity isn't a nice-to-have feature for us. It's the baseline everything else is built on.

A note from the founder

I built Glow because I went through cystic acne myself — the kind that doesn't just affect your skin, it affects how you show up in the world. I know what it's like to avoid photos, cancel plans, and feel like your skin is the first thing people see.

Glow is the tool I wish I'd had: something that helped me understand my skin instead of just judging it. That's the whole reason this exists.
